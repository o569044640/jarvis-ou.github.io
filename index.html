UBC Waypoint

Related Skills: C, VHDL / Verilog, Quartus, QSYS, De1-SoC

Check out a video of UBC Waypoint.

UBC Waypoint is a university navigation system. It serves the purpose of helping visitors and incoming students navigate through UBC with ease.

In a single device, it provides a simple touch-based interface for campus newcomers to quickly access the services they need. As a stationary device designed to be situated at various on-campus locations, it is intended to be simple to install and require minimal maintenance. With a click of a button, users can access a campus map that is GPS-calibrated according to the device’s location, and quickly navigate it using the touchscreen. Using the device’s WiFi capabilities, users can easily access safety services via the provided button-based contact options for campus security and Safewalk. Finally, to assist with UBC’s marketing efforts, UBC Waypoint also offers a handful of additional functionalities, including a simple message bulletin that can be updated via SMS, Twitter integration, basic camera functionality, and a web-based photo gallery.

UBC Waypoint is aiming to serve the UBC community and strive to make UBC a more convenient and friendly place for students, staff, and tourists to enjoy. In the future, we will aim to promote this device to all universities and anywhere in the world.

AutoPlant

Related Skills: Arduino, Python, PHP, Linux VM

Check out a video of AutoPlant.

AutoPlant is an autonomous plant caring system: a robot that takes care of your plants when you are too busy. It is assembled by a programmable robot arm, a soil moisture sensor, an ambient light sensor, a water pump, a seed container, a live-streaming camera, and LED light strips.

AutoPlant is designed to periodically probe the soil to obtain moisture level and water each plant accordingly. Also, the system monitors the ambient light level and turns on the light (the LED light strips) on cloudy days. Through a web app, the ambient light brightness and color can be adjusted to accommodate different plant type. On the web app, you can monitor and adjust how the plants are cared. The website displays all relevant sensor data, such as humidity, temperature, and light level. It also shows the water and soil moisture history of each plant. Seeds can be planted with only a single button on the website. A live camera feed on the website will let you enjoy your garden from anywhere.

Draw2gether

Related Skills: Java, XML, Android Studio, Microsoft Azure, Codacy, Jacoco

Draw2gether is a multiplayer drawing game, that allows several users to collaborate on a same piece of art. Players are given a drawing prompt and quadrant to draw, such as the top left of a tree. Afterwards, the App finds other similar drawings in the Microsoft Azure Database and stitches them together. The App contains a public gallery of all drawings. Everyone can view his/her drawings in the public gallery as well as everyone else's artworks. Inappropriate drawings can be reported to maintain a healthy drawing environment.

OS 161

Related Skills: C, Operating System

Operating System 161, as known as OS 161, is an educational operating system developed by Harvard University. It aims to assist junior computer developers to understand the underlying secrets of operating system.

During the stretch of learning OS 161, my goal was to understand key operating system concepts, such as resource virtualization, protection and concurrency. During which, I had to design my own native locking mechanisms to ensure synchronization. Also, I learned to construct the proper way to do parallel processes, file tables, system calls and virtual memory. Although some of the tasks were very challenging, I acquired a deep understanding of how an operating system interact in both hardware and software level.

Simple iPod

Related Skills: SystemVerilog, Assembly, Quartus

In CPEN 311, a course about digital system design, I designed a simple music player by writing SystemVerilog to program a De1-Soc (FPGA board) to play music that includes pausing and playing music, forward and reverse control, speed up and slow down buttons. The De1-Soc was connected to an external keyboard that is used as a controller for the iPod. The music was initially stored in the De1-Soc's flash memory, and I had to retrieve the data in bytes. The iPod is designed to read the byte chucks in the correct memory address.

  • Date: October 2017

Restaurants and Queries

Related Skills: Java, JSON Files, Database, Server

Using an excerpt from the Yelp Academic Dataset, I worked with data (in JSON format) on restaurants, and this data includes information about some restaurants, reviews of the restaurants, and user information. I constructed a unique, abstract datatype storing all these informations about restaurants.

The dataset was later used to create and maintain a simple in-memory database with restaurants, users and reviews. The database is used for a multithreading, client-server that acquires inputting queries about restaurant desire and outputs the wanted restaurant set. For example, when a user inputs a structured query containing informatioins about a preferred restaurant location, food category, price range, or review rating, a list of selected restaurants will be returned for the users to choose.
